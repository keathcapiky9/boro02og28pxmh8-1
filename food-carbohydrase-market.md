<p><p>The food carbohydrase market includes enzymes like amylase, cellulase, lactase, and pectinase, each serving distinct roles in food production. Amylase breaks down starches into sugars, enhancing sweetness in baked goods and beverages. Cellulase aids in the breakdown of cellulose, improving the texture of fruit and vegetable products. Lactase helps lactose-intolerant consumers by converting lactose into simpler sugars. Pectinase is utilized in juice and wine production to facilitate clarity and texture. Other enzymes in the market support various food processing needs.</p></p>
