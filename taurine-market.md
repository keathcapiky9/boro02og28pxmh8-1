<p><p>The taurine market comprises two primary production methods: the Ethylene Oxide Method and the Ethanol Amine Method. The Ethylene Oxide Method uses ethylene oxide as a precursor, resulting in high-purity taurine suitable for various applications, including dietary supplements and pharmaceuticals. In contrast, the Ethanol Amine Method employs ethanolamine, offering a cost-effective alternative but potentially varying in purity. Both methods cater to diverse industry demands, with applications ranging from food additives to animal feed and niche therapeutic uses.</p></p>
